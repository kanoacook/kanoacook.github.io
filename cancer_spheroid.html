Synthesis and characterization of thiol-acrylate hydrogels using a base-catalyzed Michael addition for 3D cell culture applications

I spent a summer conducting research at Lousiana State University to study an efficient method culturing breast cancer spheroids. Why? Current tumor spheroid generation is labor intensive and lacks high-throughput capabilities and and uniformity. Tumor spheroids are 3-dimensional aggregates of cancer cells that accurately mimic in vivo tumor behavior; 2-dimensional scaffolds are unable to provide the same cell-to-cell interactions.

Roles and Achievements

I led the design-of-experiment (DoE) process through researching other hydrogel synthetic procedures to prepare our novel hydrogel for the assays required for classification. Additionally, I collaborated with LSU's MatSci Department to conduct tensile strength testing, Biology Department for conducting cell line differentiation, and Chemistry Department to conduct NMR Spectroscopy scans.

The scope of the publication was focused on the synthetic chemistry of creating the novel hydrogel (link). A few years after the publication another manuscript was submitted regarding the cell culturing methods and the design of the microfluidic device. (link).
