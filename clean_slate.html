STI/UTI Detection via Microfluidics

In America, one in two sexually active individuals under the age of 25 is expected to contract an STD. The current process for STI detection takes 2-5 days to deliver results, which is anxiety inducing. Current costs for testing ranges from $50-$100. Typical point-of-care devices require invasive methods of sample extraction, and the overall process has a severe lack of privacy.

The Clean Slate microfluidic device is designed to be an rapid, discrete, and non-invasive alternative to current STI testing methods. The process is as follows:

1. 25mL of non-first pass urine is deposited into the device.

2. The device is placed on a plastic stand, and an app is launched.

3. A series of LED lights is shown onto the filter for 10 minutes. This acts a thermocouple for replicating the DNA -- think PCR!

4. A push notification will contain the results.
